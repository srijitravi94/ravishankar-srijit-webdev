FLASHBACK FRIDAY

It was five years ago on this very day that Chelsea won the Champions League.

It was five years ago, but it feels like yesterday. It was five years ago, but it feels like a lifetime ago.

Since then, we’ve won the league twice, the Europa once, fired two managers and let two more go. Jose Mourinho came back and left again. Didier Drogba came back and left again. David Luiz left and came back again. Romelu Lukaku left and is about to come back. Frank Lampard left and scored against us. Fernando Torres left and we’re all happy for him. Juan Mata left and we’re all still sad. The Old Guard is gone, the New Guard is here.

May 19, 2012, one of the most important dates in Chelsea history. The day that was written in the stars. The day that needed the most special European night at the Bridge in the comeback against Napoli. The day that needed the Miracle of the Camp Nou and the greatest goal ever scored. The day that needed a rabbit pulled out of a hat on the day itself. It was arguably the worst Chelsea team in two decades, it was arguably the greatest Chelsea team in history.
